05 / Selected Works

Architectural Projects

01
Credit: Creative Territories

Home Spa Ladkrabang

Site Ladkrabang, Bangkok
Area 280 m²
Type Residential / Spa Renovation

This project renovates a house into a private home spa while maintaining its residential function, creating a calm Japanese-inspired atmosphere through the reuse of timber from the original house to preserve memory and add value. The design introduces a gradual transition from exterior to interior through wooden walkways and semi-outdoor spaces connected to a central courtyard, allowing natural light and nature to enhance a sense of relaxation. Reclaimed wood is used in key elements such as roof extensions, walkways, and fences, placed where it can be seen and touched, combined with natural materials to create a simple, warm, and tranquil living environment.

02
Credit: Creative Territories

Prachoom Chang

Site Si Phraya Rd, Bang Rak, Bangkok
Area 1,600 m²
Type Heritage Renovation / Creative Space

The Prachoom Chang project is a renovation of a nearly century-old building, transforming it into a space that brings together the identities of three creative disciplines — fashion, furniture, and art. The spatial arrangement emphasises separation for expression and connection for dialogue, where each zone maintains its own identity through materials, atmosphere, and function. Architecturally, the project preserves traces of the existing building as a backdrop for storytelling, while introducing new elements in a deliberate and contrasting way — creating a dialogue between past and present.

Brutalism Meets Craft
Fashion Studio — Philip Huang

A design approach that blends the raw clarity of Brutalism with the intimacy of craftsmanship. It emphasises fundamental materials — concrete, steel, and glass — revealing imperfection and the passage of time. Spaces are simple, functional, and alive, evolving through use. It prioritises structure, clarity, and material honesty over decoration, with nature introduced subtly. Light, texture, and textiles shape atmosphere and spatial experience. Overall, it is architecture that is honest, minimal, yet profound — embracing imperfection, aging, and seeing them as the true beauty of living.

Ⅱ
Preserve & Reveal
Furniture Studio — PARIBAWGA

The project is grounded in the idea of 'preserve and reveal,' where the existing structure, walls, and traces of time are kept and expressed as they are. The original building becomes a living backdrop, allowing materials and craftsmanship to stand out naturally. The space is reduced to only what is essential, allowing the wooden furniture to take the main role. Showroom, private kitchen, and office are seamlessly connected, creating a fluid spatial experience that feels calm, minimal, yet rich in subtle details.

Ⅲ
Bare but Not Empty
Art Studio — Channatip Studio

A studio defined by material honesty, exposed yet calm and composed. The programme is distilled to its essentials, allowing art to emerge freely without obstruction. Natural light and passive ventilation are embedded as architectural drivers, with light moving through the space to reveal surface, dust, pigment, and time. This is not a space designed for perfection, but one that embraces imperfection — including stains, traces, and transformation — as part of its aesthetic. It is a studio that is bare but not empty, minimal yet profound, a space where art breathes through architecture.

03
Thesis Project

Suphan Buri Folk Music Center

Site Banharn-Jamsai Tower, Chalerm Phatthara Ratchini Park
Area 3,458 m²
Type Cultural / Performance Facility

This thesis project aims to promote, preserve, and innovate Suphan Buri's folk music, which has been recognised by UNESCO as a Creative City of Music. The design envisions a cultural space that fosters interaction between people and music, using architecture as a medium to connect the past with the present through areas for learning, performance, and musical creation. Curved lines are used to create a continuous rhythmic flow, while local materials — including bamboo and terracotta — are employed to reflect the regional identity and cultural heritage of Suphanburi.

04
Academic Project

Yodpiman's Development Project

Site Yodpiman Market, Bangkok
Area 4,000 m²
Type Hotel / Mixed-Use Development

Building a hotel within a flower market poses challenges related to space and perspective, with the overarching concept focusing on creating a romantic ambiance. To achieve architectural harmony, the design incorporates wooden materials and open spaces, preserving the charm and ambiance of the Yodpiman Flower Market. The massing is divided into two zones — 'Live' and 'Learn' — where the learning space is oriented toward the market, creating an organic connection between the hotel and the existing flower market. The romantic concept emphasises privacy, with the hotel interior introducing a fresh perspective separated from the main market activity.

05
Academic Project

Bali Hai Terminal Station

Site Bali Hai, Pattaya, Chonburi
Area 7,800 m²
Type Transit / Cultural Infrastructure

The Bali Hai Monorail is a newly developed project with a visionary extension to transform the station into a cinematic experience space — as Pattaya has been recognised by UNESCO as a Creative City of Film. The design takes advantage of the city's hilly terrain and scenic views to create a station façade that also serves as an open-air movie viewing area. The façade features a dynamic structural design incorporating axis twisting and distinctive visual angles to enhance the project's identity. Climate simulation across seasonal conditions informed the adaptive façade system balancing natural light, heat control, and transparency.

06
Installation / NRCT

Bangkok Design Week 2024
— Rubberwood Pavilion

Site NRCT, Bangkok
Type Pavilion / Structural Installation

Developed under the redevelopment project of an old building into a learning centre at the National Research Council of Thailand (NRCT), this pavilion explores rubberwood as a modern architectural material. Rubberwood was chosen for its affordability and quality as a Thai timber. In designing the model, a repeating modular system was developed to create an industrial production methodology. The entrance arch form demonstrates the bending potential of rubberwood, while the flexible zone arrangement allows the pavilion to host workshops, events, and exhibitions within the same structure.

07
Structural Research

Structural Journey 2023

Tutor Prof. Kaoru Suehiro
Institution Kyushu University
Type Material Research / Prototype

A structural material research project guided by Professor Kaoru Suehiro of Kyushu University, exploring typological interlocking material systems. The research investigated a modular approach where individual units connect without conventional joints — no glue, nuts, or nails — using folding angles designed into each unit's geometry. The interlocking connection allows each unit to be folded, broken, or bent, and when assembled, forms emerge as free-form structures. Two experimental models were developed: the first without overlapping interfaces, the second mixing two types of arrangement with nested interfaces to enable structural self-support through the merger principle.

08
International Collaboration

International Architectural Workshop 2023

Location Wat Sai Floating Market, Bangkok
Partners Kyushu University & BECAT University
Duration 23–29 Mar 2023

Wat Sai Floating Market encompasses a diverse array of lifestyles — residents, merchants, and customers — interconnected by multiple travel routes both on water and land. This workshop analysed the area by re-zoning the market into four phases, examining the interplay between open and closed spaces. By linking each area and identifying all possibilities for new pathways, the team developed a proposal introducing new activities that attract visitors while reinforcing the existing community fabric. The proposal creates a new spatial layer that promotes closed spaces including new activities capable of drawing more people into the market environment.
